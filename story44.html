The Thief Who Got Stolen From

A nimble thief slipped into a house to steal a purse but tripped, leaving the purse behind as he fled. The homeowner discovered the attempt and called Mulla.

Instead of calls for punishment, Mulla invited the thief to breakfast and offered him work repairing nets. He explained that shame and hunger drive small crimes.

The thief took the work and, grateful, turned his skill to mending rather than stealing. Over time he became a steady helper and a symbol of how kindness can redirect a life.

Moral: Kindness reforms where punishment might not.
