The Guest at His Own House

Returning after months away, Mulla found his family greeting him as if he were a visitor, with songs and a feast prepared. No one expected a homecoming to feel like hospitality.

Mulla accepted the celebration with gentle humor, telling tales of his travels and offering compliments to those who had held the home together.

The family realized they had grown used to routine and had forgotten to cherish presence. From then on, small returns were marked with care and gratitude.

Moral: Value presence as you would a guest.
