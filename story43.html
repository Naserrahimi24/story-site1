The Clothes Make the Man

A well-dressed traveler received deference while a ragged scholar was ignored. The scholar's words fell on deaf ears despite their wisdom.

Mulla exchanged their cloaks for a day and watched how treatment followed the garment rather than the person. The villagers felt ashamed of their quick judgments.

They resolved to train themselves to look beyond fabric, and the traveler and scholar both earned the respect due to their heart and mind.

Moral: Judge by character, not cloth.
